Professional Code of Ethics

Version 1.0 · Effective August 1, 2025 · ISSLA Risk Global Services LLC

Home / Code of Ethics

Purpose & Scope

This Code of Ethics governs the professional conduct of all persons operating under the ISSLA Risk mandate. It applies to full-time and part-time staff, field analysts and research partners, intelligence analysts, strategic partners and collaborators, independent contractors, and third-party service providers.

Compliance with this Code is a condition of engagement. Violations may result in immediate contract termination, referral to law enforcement, or permanent exclusion from future engagements.

01

Absolute Integrity

All information gathered, analyzed, and reported must reflect actual findings. Fabrication, misrepresentation, or selective omission of material facts is prohibited under any circumstances.

02

Legality and Compliance

All intelligence activities must comply with applicable laws in every jurisdiction of operation. Illegal methods of data collection, unauthorized access, and non-compliant surveillance are strictly prohibited.

03

Confidentiality

Client identities, inquiry scope, and deliverable content must be protected throughout and after every engagement. Information is shared only on a strict need-to-know basis within the authorized engagement team.

04

Objectivity and Impartiality

Intelligence analysis must be fact-based and free from personal bias, political influence, or financial motivation beyond the scope of the engagement. Findings must reflect evidence, not desired outcomes.

05

Conflict of Interest Prevention

All potential conflicts of interest — existing or prospective — must be disclosed to the Ethics Committee before engagement. Staff may not accept engagements where personal interests could compromise the integrity of the work.

06

Respect for Human Rights

ISSLA Risk explicitly rejects any form of torture, harassment, discrimination, or coercive conduct in the course of intelligence operations. All activities must respect the fundamental rights and dignity of individuals.

07

Ethical Use of Resources and Technology

Technology and analytical tools must be used only for authorized purposes. Unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data is strictly prohibited. All digital activities must comply with applicable cybersecurity and data protection laws.

08

Professional Conduct in the Field

All field activities must be conducted with discretion, cultural sensitivity, and professional judgment. Operatives represent ISSLA Risk in every interaction and must uphold the firm's standards in all circumstances.

09

Rejection of Sanctioned Entities

ISSLA Risk does not engage with individuals or entities listed on OFAC, UN, EU, or other applicable sanctions lists. All clients and subjects of inquiry are screened against current sanctions databases prior to engagement.

Reporting Ethics Violations

Ethics violations or concerns should be reported to the Ethics Committee at ethics@isslarisk.com. All reports are treated with strict confidentiality. ISSLA Risk guarantees no retaliation against any person who reports a violation in good faith.

Validity and Acceptance

This Code of Ethics is effective as of August 1, 2025 (Version 1.0). All staff, contractors, and partners are required to acknowledge and sign this Code as a condition of engagement with ISSLA Risk Global Services LLC.

Operations are conducted with accuracy, integrity, and accountability in accordance with these principles.

Navigating Complex Risks with Actionable Intelligence

Intelligence services provided do not constitute legal, regulatory, or compliance counsel.

ISSLA Risk is a corporate intelligence consulting and advisory firm. We do not operate as a licensed private investigation agency.
