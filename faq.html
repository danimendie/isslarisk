About ISSLA Risk

ISSLA Risk is a specialist corporate intelligence and risk advisory firm. We provide verified intelligence products to multinational corporations, private equity funds, legal practices, family offices, and government-adjacent entities. Our work focuses on four disciplines: global due diligence, background analysis, reputation analysis, and country risk & political forecasting.

Our headquarters is located in Albuquerque, New Mexico, USA. We also operate a regional office in Panama City, Republic of Panama, which supports our Latin American and Caribbean intelligence operations. Both offices can be reached at contact@isslarisk.com.

ISSLA Risk covers North America, the Caribbean, and South America. We have particular expertise in markets characterized by elevated opacity, political risk, or complex regulatory environments — including the United States, Canada, Panama, Colombia, Brazil, Argentina, Peru, Mexico, and Caribbean offshore jurisdictions such as the BVI and Cayman Islands. Founded in 2009, we have built specialized source networks across these regions over more than fifteen years of operational activity.

Our Services

We offer four core intelligence services:

  • Global Due Diligence — Pre-investment partner and vendor vetting, available in three tiers (Essential, Comprehensive, and Executive).
  • Background Analysis — Deep professional and personal background research on individuals for high-stakes appointments or partnerships.
  • Reputation Analysis — Structured assessment of how a subject entity or individual is perceived across markets, media, and stakeholder networks.
  • Country Risk & Political Forecast — Geopolitical and regulatory risk assessments for market entry, operational decisions, or investment committees.

Our clients include multinational corporations, private equity and venture capital funds, legal and law firms, family offices, financial institutions, insurance and reinsurance companies, real estate developers, energy companies, and government-adjacent entities. Our common thread is clients whose decisions carry significant financial, legal, or reputational consequences — and who require verified intelligence rather than speculation.

Process & Methodology

Our methodology is built on three intelligence layers: OSINT (Open Source Intelligence), HUMINT (Human Intelligence), and SOCMIN (Social Media Intelligence). OSINT covers structured research of public records, registries, filings, and media archives. HUMINT involves our regional source networks providing ground-level verification. SOCMIN analyses digital footprints, professional profiles, and online behavioral patterns. Every significant finding requires independent corroboration before it appears in your report.

Turnaround depends on the scope and type of engagement. An Essential due diligence report typically delivers within 5–7 business days. Comprehensive reports generally require 10–15 business days. Executive-level mandates, multi-subject engagements, and country risk assessments are scoped and scheduled individually. We can discuss expedited delivery for time-sensitive situations — contact us to discuss your specific timeline.

No finding reaches your report without independent corroboration. We apply a two-source minimum standard for every significant claim — meaning every substantive conclusion is verified through at least two independent, non-derivative sources. Where a claim cannot be independently verified, it is either excluded or clearly labeled as unverified and attributed to its single source. This standard is non-negotiable across all engagement tiers.

Confidentiality & Security

Client confidentiality is the foundation of our practice. We do not confirm or deny the existence of any client relationship. All engagement details, deliverables, and communications are treated as strictly confidential. We do not use client information for any secondary purpose, do not discuss mandates with third parties, and do not reference past clients in marketing materials without explicit written authorization. All staff operate under binding confidentiality agreements.

Yes. ISSLA Risk operates in full compliance with the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the United States Privacy Act, and applicable local data protection regulations in each jurisdiction where we operate. All research is conducted using lawful bases, and personal data is processed strictly for the purpose of the specific engagement. For full details, please review our Privacy Policy.

All deliverables are transmitted through secure, encrypted channels. We do not transmit sensitive reports via unencrypted email as standard practice. Specific secure delivery arrangements are confirmed with each client at the outset of the engagement. If you have existing secure communication protocols you prefer, we can adapt to them.

Reports & Deliverables

Report structure varies by service type and tier, but all ISSLA Risk reports include: an executive summary designed for decision-maker consumption, detailed findings organized by risk category, source attribution for every substantive claim, a risk rating or assessment conclusion, and actionable recommendations. Reports are professionally formatted and designed to be presented directly to investment committees, legal counsel, or boards without editorial modification.

Starting an Engagement

The simplest way to begin is via our contact form or by emailing contact@isslarisk.com. We will respond within one business day to schedule a confidential briefing call. During that call, we define scope, timeline, deliverables, and commercial terms. No sensitive details about the subject of investigation are required at the inquiry stage — we only need to understand the broad nature of the mandate to provide an initial assessment.

At the outset of an engagement, we require: the identity of the subject (individual or entity), the jurisdiction(s) of primary interest, the purpose of the investigation (investment, compliance, litigation support, etc.), and any specific risk concerns you want prioritized. The more context you provide, the better we can tailor the investigation. Supplementary documents — corporate filings, prior reports, correspondence — are always welcome and treated as strictly confidential.
